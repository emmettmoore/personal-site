Hello! My name is Emmett, and I live in Somerville, MA.

I spent my childhood in Houston, TX, but moved to the greater Boston area just in time for my angsty teenage years.

I graduated from Tufts University in 2015 with a BS in Computer Science. Growing up, both my parents were programmers. It was only natural that I would try to avoid my fate for a semester or two, but I eventually came to follow in their footsteps. After taking one class, I was hooked. There are a lot of great things about computer science, but the thing I love the most is the opportunity to use technical skills to solve tangible problems that are important to me.

While in school, I discovered the best sport in the world. I am obviously talking about rock climbing. Climbing requires persistence and creativity, and I found that as I grew to become a better climber, I also learned how to think for myself. I have tried to apply that skill to all facets of my life with varying degrees of success. Over the years I have happily let climbing drag me to some of the most beautiful places in the world and introduce me to many of my best friends along the way.

Since graduation, I have applied my interesting in programming to my job as a full stack software engineer at InsightSquared.

When I am not working or climbing, I am usually:

  • Riding my 1980-something Univega around the city until the next thing eventually breaks and I stubbornly try to fix it myself.
  • Working on this site, or various other projects.
  • Listening to new music (I used to play, now I just listen, but maybe someday I'll get back into playing again).
  • Outside.
  • Playing with my (parents') dogs.
  • Reading.
  • Watching the Red Sox.
